Rotary Positional Embeddings (RoPE) and Rotational Logic
RoPE is a vital component of the Gemma architecture used by SAGE-7, enabling the model to incorporate sequence order into self-attention mechanisms without mixing position and semantic signals in the hidden state. Instead of adding a positional vector to the word embedding, RoPE applies a rotation matrix to the queries (Q) and keys (K).
The technical implementation of RoPE breaks the hidden state vector into 2-dimensional chunks, rotating each chunk by an angle \theta proportional to the token's position m. For any two tokens at positions m and n, the attention weight depends on the relative distance m-n, making the mechanism inherently aware of proximity.
The SAGE-7 system advances this through CARoPE (Context-Aware RoPE), which dynamically generates head-specific frequency patterns conditioned on the input tokens.
